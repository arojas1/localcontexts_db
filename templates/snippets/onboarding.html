1/6 Your profile

The profile enables you to join multiple accounts on the hub. You can create accounts on behalf of your Indigenous community, institution or as a researcher. In the profile section, you can access all the different accounts you are a member of.

2/6 Accounts

There are three account types: Indigenous communities, institutions, and researchers.

Depending on the account you have, you can develop Labels or Notices.

3/6 Local Contexts Projects

Local Contexts Projects assist in documenting cultural material on the hub. Institutions and researchers can apply Notices to it, and Indigenous communities can apply their customized Labels.

There are various types of Projects, such as collections, items, expeditions, exhibitions, or samples.

4/6 Labels

The Label descriptions can be customized by Indigenous communities to ensure that their local and traditional protocols are embedded in those Labels.

When Indigenous communities apply those Labels to relevant Projects, the Labels will be used as an identifier to define how their cultural material is accessed and used.

5/6 Notices

Institutions and researchers can create Projects for cultural materials they protect and apply Notices to them.

By applying notices it will assist Indigenous Communities to realise there is Indigenous interests in these collections and data.

6/6 Members

Community and Institutional accounts can have as many members as necessary. There are three types of roles within the hub that allow different activites and permissions they are:

  • Administrators
  • Editors
  • Viewers
To learn more about them, click on the 'Members' tab on your account dashboard.
