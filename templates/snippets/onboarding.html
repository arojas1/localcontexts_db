1/6 Your profile

The profile you create enables you to join multiple accounts on the hub and create accounts on behalf of your community or institution.

You will be able to access all the different accounts you are a member of from your profile.

2/6 Accounts

There are three types of accounts within the hub these accounts are specifically for Indigenous Communities, Institutions, and Researchers.

Depending on which account you are a member of you could be developing Labels or Notices.

3/6 Local Contexts Projects

Local Contexts Projects assist in documenting cultural material on the hub. Institutions and researchers can apply Notices to it, and Indigenous Communities can apply their customized Labels.

There are various types of projects, such as collections, items, expeditions, exhibitions, or samples.

4/6 Labels

Through the Local Contexts Hub, Indigenous Communities can ensure that their local and traditional protocols are applied to TK & BC Labels to determine how their cultural material is accessed and used going forward.

Community members can customize the template text of the TK & BC Labels in the hub to outline their specific local protocols.

5/6 Notices

Institutions and researchers can apply Notices to cultural materials they currently protect using the Local contexts Hub.

The Notices are specific tools for institutions and researchers which support the recognition of Indigenous interests in collections and data.

6/6 Members

Community and Institutional accounts can have as many members as necessary. There are three types of responsibilities available to each account: Administrator, Editors and Viewers

There are different permissions associated with each role. To learn more about them, click on the 'Members' tab in the account dashboard.
